How to Install or Replace
a Bicycle Saddle

1- Unscrew the clamp bolts on the bottom of the saddle.
2- Lift and turn the top clamp to remove the saddle.
3- Clean the dirt and residue off the clamp with a damp paper towel.
4- Grease the bolts and new saddle rails with bike lube.
5- Set the saddle rails in the bottom clamp's channels.
6- Reposition the top clamp so it fits over the rails.
7- Tighten the clamp bolts to hold the saddle in position.

How to Adjust Bike Brakes

1- Test the tightness of your cables by pulling each brake lever.
2- Loosen the barrel adjusters for minor brake cable adjustments.
3- Unscrew the bolt holding the brake cable on the caliper.
4- Pull the brake cable outward to tighten it.
5- Tighten the bolt holding the brake cable on the caliper.
6- Tighten the barrel adjusters on the handlebars.

How to Fix a Slipped Bicycle Chain

1- Downshift bike into lowest front gear (using the left shifter).
2- Lift the chain and put it back on the chain ring.
3- Put the chain on the outer chain guide of the chain breaker.
4- Remove the chain breaker by backing up the drive pin.
5- To rejoin the chain, align the open ends of the chain.
6- Wiggle the link you just reattached. If it's too stiff, continue on to the next part.

How to Fix a Flat Bike Tire

1- Release your brakes, then the wheel.
2- Deflate the tube completely and remove it by pressing on the bead
(edge) of the tire or using a tire lever so you can access the tube.
3- Inspect the wheel, tire and tube for the location and causes of tears or punctures.
4- When out riding, you may prefer to replace the tube with a new one and fix the damaged one later.
